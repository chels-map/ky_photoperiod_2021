What is photoperiod?

Photoperiod is the duration of daylight a crop experiences per day and is an important driver of crop physiological processes, especially flowering. For example, many cool-season vegetable crops that are planted in early Spring will begin to flower (also called "bolting") when photoperiods exceed 12 hours. These cool-season crops are long-day plants, meaning they flower when days become longer.
